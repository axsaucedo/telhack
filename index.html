One of the world's largest initiatives to revolutionise healthcare education through a hackathon

London | 6-8 November 2015



TELHack is Health Education England's premier hackathon aimed at transforming healthcare education

TELHack will bring together 80 of the UK’s best developers to create new technologies for healthcare education positioning the UK as the leading country for innovation in healthcare education. A Hack Partners event which is being run under the NHS Health Education England (HEE) Technology Enhanced Learning (TEL) programme.

TELHack will take place in a unique immersive venue in London in November 2015. The hackathon will bring together technology giants and public bodies such as the NHS and HEE to innovate in healthcare education.

We said ‘Let’s do this!’ and we did!

TELHack is Health Education England's flagship hackathon aimed at innovating healthcare education. A leading event bringing together 80 of the best developers, designers and industry experts from all around the UK to conceive, build and launch high impact solutions that will revolutionise healthcare education.

An explosion of energy, enthusiasm, creativity and hard work (working through the nights!) created a really incredible atmosphere that was maintained through the whole event. From initial pitches based around the challenges set by HEE and sponsors RCNi, teams were quickly formed and set to work until Sunday evening when they had working prototypes ready to present to a panel of judges.


We were lucky enough to have as judges:

  • Alan Ryan, HEE TEL Lead and HEE e-LfH National Programme Director
  • Steve Clarke, Deputy Chief Executive of HEE and Director of Finance
  • Jan Jilis (JJ) van Delson, RCNi, Sales and Marketing Director
  • Toby Stone, CEO Newsquare Innovation

Some of the really exciting ideas developed included:

  • “Score my Sutures” – an image recognition tool to help surgeons in training practise their suturing with immediate assessment and feedback.
  • “Echo” – a project using iBeacon technology to help trainees, students and locums in clinical settings with orientation and information.
  • “Geyedlines” – a prototype product using Google Glass to present key clinical information to clinicians in real time at the point of care.
  • “iNurse” – a quiz-based, interactive learning webapp for training nurses using gamification to increase interest and motivation.
  • “Ward Words” – an interactive, audio-visual app for nurses with English as a second language needing to build confidence and language in clinical settings.

Alan Ryan, Head of the Technology Enhanced Learning Programme at HEE said:

"The NHS and healthcare education needs to keep pace in a fast-changing world. We need to look for innovative ways of working, not only with people in healthcare education but from other sectors and industries."

"The 'TEL Hack' was exactly the kind of event where we could build strong relationships between technologists, developers, designers and those of us working to improve how we deliver healthcare education and training. It was about bringing together bright and motivated people with different skills, different experience and perspectives to focus on some of the real challenges that the NHS faces.”


Everything has to come to an end

TELHack concluded with participants presenting their creative projects on the Sunday evening. A follow up showcase event took place at The National Science Museum in London on 10 November to explore how HEE can support some of these ideas going forward.


Alan added:

“We will now work with mentor NHS Trusts and hospitals to further explore the ongoing viability of some of these exciting projects in the NHS and further opportunities to use innovative technology to enhance and improve healthcare education and training.”

“The teams, giving up their time for free, developed their working prototypes in key areas such as providing feedback in clinical settings, finding ways to induct people quickly in new environments and ways in which to build knowledge and skills on-the-job. ”

Challenges

Big data in health

In healthcare education there are heaps of data available. This spawns a sea of posibilities around big data analytics. You can make use of tools like elastic search, hadoop, apache spark, apache mesos, between many other big data analytics tools for this!

This challenge aims to see solutions around large datasets, making these usable and readable, allowing for more practical analytics.

Digital Literacy

There are a large amount of tools available to enhance education within health. One of the largest problems is that often, healthcare professionals don't know these tools exist, or when they get their hands on them, they might come by as too complex.

This challenge aims to see solutions around digital literacy, and how you can decrease the gap between technology literacy and healthcare education.

Learning Environments

Healthcare education relies on unique and immersive learning environments to have a positive impact in the learning process. It is often expensive and challening to achieve this, however with new cutting edge technology, there are affordable, more efficient ways to achieve this.

This challenge aims to see solutions around innovative learning environments, and how you can take advantage of latest technologies to achieve it.

Feedback

Feedback collection and analytics in learning environments is a very revisited problem within healthcare education. With the rise of internet of things, there has been a great opportunity to innovate in this area, gathering feedback in new ways and displaying it in intuitive interfaces.

This challenge aims to see solutions around around feedback - innovative ways of collecting feedback, intuitive interfaces for feedback, etc.
